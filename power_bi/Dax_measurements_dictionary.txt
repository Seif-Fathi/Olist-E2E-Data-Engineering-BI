# DAX Measures & Business Intelligence Manual

## 1. Financial & Volume KPIs

1 - Total Sales
DAX Formula: Total Sales = SUM(v_fact_order_items[price])

Business Question Answered: "What is the total revenue generated by the platform from product sales?"

Target Departments / Stakeholders: Executive Management (C-Suite), Finance Department.

2 - Total Orders
DAX Formula: Total Orders = DISTINCTCOUNT(v_fact_orders[order_id])

Business Question Answered: "How many unique orders have been successfully placed on the platform?"

Target Departments / Stakeholders: Operations, Sales.

3 - Total Items Sold
DAX Formula: Total Items Sold = COUNT(v_fact_order_items[order_item_id])

Business Question Answered: "How many physical items or products were actually fulfilled and dispatched from the warehouses?"

Target Departments / Stakeholders: Inventory/Warehouse Management, Logistics.

4 - Average Order Value (AOV)
DAX Formula: Average Order Value = DIVIDE([Total Sales], [Total Orders], 0)

Business Question Answered: "On average, how much does a customer spend per order?"

Target Departments / Stakeholders: Marketing (for designing Upselling and Bundling strategies), Sales.

5 - Gross Profit 
DAX Formula: Gross Profit = [Total Sales] - [Total Freight Cost]

Business Question Answered: "What is the net revenue remaining from product sales after accounting for platform-incurred shipping and logistics costs?"

Target Departments / Stakeholders: Finance Department, Supply Chain Operations, Executive Management (C-Suite).

6 - Gross Profit Margin %
DAX Formula:
Gross Profit Margin % = DIVIDE([Gross Profit], [Total Sales], 0)

Business Question Answered: "How efficiently is the platform retaining profit from sales? What percentage of each dollar generated represents actual profit after deduction of freight values?"

Target Departments / Stakeholders: Executive Management (C-Suite), Finance Department, Pricing Strategy Team.

## 2. Customer Behavior & Growth Analytics

1 - Basket Size (Items per Order)
DAX Formula: Items per Order = DIVIDE([Total Items Sold], [Total Orders], 0)

Business Question Answered: "On average, how many items does a customer place in their shopping cart per order?"

Target Departments / Stakeholders: Marketing (for designing coupon campaigns and driving sales volume), UI/UX Team (for optimizing the checkout experience).

2 - Sales per Customer (CLV Concept)
DAX Formula: 

Sales per Customer = 
VAR TotalUniqueCustomers = DISTINCTCOUNT(v_fact_orders[customer_id])
RETURN
DIVIDE([Total Sales], TotalUniqueCustomers, 0)

Business Question Answered: "What is the average financial value that a single unique customer represents to the platform?"

Target Departments / Stakeholders: Customer Relationship Management (CRM), Marketing (for defining Customer Acquisition Cost - CAC budgets).

## 3. Operations, Logistics & Shipping 

1 - Total Freight Cost
DAX Formula: Total Freight Cost = SUM(v_fact_order_items[freight_value])

Business Question Answered: "What is the total cost incurred for parcel shipping and logistics?"

Target Departments / Stakeholders: Shipping & Logistics Department, Finance Department.

2 - Freight to Sales Ratio
DAX Formula: Freight to Sales Ratio = DIVIDE([Total Freight Cost], [Total Sales], 0)

Business Question Answered: "Is shipping cost eating into profit margins? What percentage of the product price does shipping represent?"

Target Departments / Stakeholders: Marketing and Finance (for product pricing and free shipping promotion strategies).

3 - Avg Delivery Days
DAX Formula:

Avg Delivery Days = 
CALCULATE(
    AVERAGEX(
        v_fact_orders,
        DATEDIFF(v_fact_orders[purchase_date], v_fact_orders[delivered_to_customer_at], DAY)
    ),
    NOT(ISBLANK(v_fact_orders[delivered_to_customer_at])),
    CROSSFILTER(v_fact_order_items[order_id], v_fact_orders[order_id], BOTH)
)


Business Question Answered: "On average, how many days does an order take from the moment of purchase until it reaches the customer's doorstep?"

Target Departments / Stakeholders: Logistics and Supply Chain teams (for evaluating shipping carrier performance).

4 - Delayed Orders %

DAX Formula:
Delayed Orders % = 
VAR DelayedCount = 
    CALCULATE(
        [Total Orders],
        FILTER(
            v_fact_orders,
            v_fact_orders[delivered_to_customer_at] > v_fact_orders[estimated_delivery_at]
        )
    )
RETURN
DIVIDE(DelayedCount, [Total Orders], 0)

Business Question Answered: "What percentage of orders were delivered late compared to the estimated delivery date promised to the customer?"

Target Departments / Stakeholders: Quality Management, Customer Support (for forecasting complaint volumes).

## 4. Customer Satisfaction & Quality

1 - Total Reviews
DAX Formula: Total Reviews = COUNT(v_fact_reviews[review_id])

Business Question Answered: "What is the volume of customer engagement with the feedback and review system?"

Target Departments / Stakeholders: Customer Support, Platform Management.

2 - Average Review Score
DAX Formula: 

Avg Review Score = 
CALCULATE(
    AVERAGE(v_fact_reviews[review_score]),
    CROSSFILTER(v_fact_orders[order_id], v_fact_order_items[order_id], Both)
)

**Note:** 
"Utilized the CROSSFILTER function to temporarily enable a bi-directional (Both) relationship within the measure. This allows the filter context to flow 
from the Orders table to the Order Items table without permanently enabling it in the Data Model, thereby maintaining optimal performance and preventing ambiguity or loops in circular relationships between Fact tables."


Business Question Answered: "What is the overall rating for customer satisfaction regarding products and the purchasing experience (out of 5 stars)?"

Target Departments / Stakeholders: Product Management, Vendor Relations Managers.

3 - Positive Reviews %
DAX Formula: 
Positive Reviews % = 
DIVIDE(
    CALCULATE([Total Reviews], v_fact_reviews[review_score] >= 4),
    [Total Reviews],
    0
) 

Business Question Answered: "What percentage of customers are fully satisfied (4 or 5 stars) with their experience?"

Target Departments / Stakeholders: Marketing (for social proof in advertising), Executive Management (C-Suite).

## 5. Marketplace & Seller Analytics 

1 - Active Sellers
DAX Formula: Active Sellers = DISTINCTCOUNT(v_fact_order_items[seller_id])

Business Question Answered: "How many active sellers have made actual sales during this period?"

Target Departments / Stakeholders: Business Development (BD) Team, Vendor Management.

2 - Average Sales per Seller
DAX Formula: Avg Sales per Seller = DIVIDE([Total Sales], [Active Sellers], 0)

Business Question Answered: "What is the average sales volume generated per seller on our platform?"

Target Departments / Stakeholders: Vendor Management (to ensure seller retention and platform loyalty).

## 6. Time Intelligence

1 - Sales Last Year (LY)
DAX Formula: 
Sales Last Year = 
CALCULATE(
    [Total Sales],
    SAMEPERIODLASTYEAR(dim_date[date_key])
)

Business Question Answered: "What were our sales for the exact same day/month/quarter last year?"

Target Departments / Stakeholders: Strategic Planning, Finance Department.

2 - YoY Growth %
DAX Formula:

YoY Growth % = 
VAR CurrentSales = [Total Sales]
VAR PastSales = [Sales Last Year]
RETURN
DIVIDE(CurrentSales - PastSales, PastSales, 0)

Business Question Answered: "What is the year-over-year sales growth rate compared to last year? Are we scaling or declining?"

Target Departments / Stakeholders: Executive Management (C-Suite), Investors, Finance, Marketing.

3 - Sales MTD (Month-To-Date)
DAX Formula: Sales MTD = TOTALMTD([Total Sales], dim_date[date_key])

Business Question Answered: "What are our cumulative sales from the beginning of the current month up to the current date?"

Target Departments / Stakeholders: Daily Sales Team, Management (for monitoring monthly target achievements in real-time).
